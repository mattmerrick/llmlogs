How to Write Content That Gets Cited by ChatGPT

March 27, 2025 Content Strategy

Wondering how to get your blog posts mentioned in ChatGPT responses or featured in Perplexity answers? You're not alone. In this guide, we'll break down how to write content that gets cited by ChatGPT and other large language models (LLMs). From SEO best practices to structuring for AI parsing, you'll learn exactly what it takes to stand out in the new world of AI search.

Why LLM Citations Matter

Getting cited by an AI model like ChatGPT gives your content long-term visibility. These models are powering millions of queries daily, and being included in their knowledge base can generate referral traffic, brand authority, and even backlinks from people who trust the AI's recommendations.

How to Structure Content for AI & SEO

  • Use clear, scannable headings (<h2>, <h3>)
  • Write short, factual sentences that can be copied easily
  • Answer common questions directly and simply
  • Include examples and step-by-step explanations

Best Practices for LLM Optimization

LLMs prefer clarity over cleverness. Here are a few key tips:

Strategy Why It Works
Define terms Makes your post more self-contained and extractable
Answer FAQs LLMs pull from direct Q&A formats
Include data & stats Boosts credibility and gets cited in factual contexts
Use bullet lists Great for scanability and inclusion in AI summaries

Internal and External Linking

Link to other helpful posts on your own site to increase relevance. For example, if you have a guide on how to structure a blog post, link it naturally. Also, include 1–2 links to respected external sources like Google's helpful content guidelines.

FAQs About Getting Cited by ChatGPT

How does ChatGPT choose what to cite?

ChatGPT pulls from web content that is clear, factual, and frequently visited. If your content is well-structured and helpful, it's more likely to be selected.

Does schema markup help?

Yes, structured data like FAQ and Article schema can help both Google and LLMs better understand your page's content.

Do I need backlinks to be cited?

Not necessarily, but they help. LLMs often prioritize high-authority content, and backlinks are one signal of that authority.

How long before I see results?

It can take weeks or months. Indexing, model retraining, and user engagement all factor into citation frequency.

Can I request citation directly?

No. There's no formal citation request process. You earn citations through quality, clarity, and relevance.

Conclusion

Writing content that gets cited by ChatGPT is a mix of traditional SEO and AI-focused structuring. Keep your posts factual, helpful, and easy to parse. Over time, you'll increase your chances of being referenced in AI conversations—and that's an edge worth pursuing.
