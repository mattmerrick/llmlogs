Structured Data

Learn how to implement structured data to improve your content's visibility in LLM responses.

What is Structured Data?

Structured data is a standardized format for providing information about a page and classifying its content. It helps LLMs better understand your content by:

  • Providing clear context about your content
  • Defining relationships between different elements
  • Specifying content types and attributes
  • Making information more accessible to AI models

Implementation Methods

There are several ways to implement structured data:

  • JSON-LD (Recommended)
  • Microdata
  • RDFa

JSON-LD is the preferred format for LLM optimization as it's easier to maintain and less prone to errors.

Common Schema Types

Use these schema types to improve your content's visibility:

  • Article - For blog posts and articles
  • HowTo - For step-by-step guides
  • FAQPage - For question and answer content
  • Product - For product information
  • Organization - For company details

Best Practices

Follow these guidelines for effective structured data implementation:

  • Use the most specific schema type possible
  • Include all required properties
  • Validate your markup before publishing
  • Keep your structured data up to date
  • Test with different LLM models
